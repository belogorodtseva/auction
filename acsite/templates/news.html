Average Brit spends nine months of their life bargain hunting, study finds

Adults typically invest two hours a week in search of discounts

more

The average Brit spends the equivalent of almost nine months of their life bargain-hunting - saving more than £6,200 year, a study has found.

Adults typically spend two hours-a-week searching for a good deal or cut price items - the equivalent of around four days and eight hours in total over the average year.

Bargain hunters estimate they save £6,247.93 annually through a combination of auctions, discount vouchers, price comparison sites, regular visits to the supermarket reduced aisle and more.

Eager for a great deal, one fifth have adopted an 'early bird catches the worm' mentality and queued in the early hours so they can nab the best bargains before anyone else.
